Before the Saudi rule, the conditions in the regions

Northen Saudi Arabia:

Before the unification of the Kingdom, the situation in the northern regions of Saudi Arabia was diverse and varied over different historical periods. There were independent tribes managing their internal affairs in some areas, while the Saudi Arabian Kingdom was being established in the southern part of the Arabian Peninsula under the rule of the Al Saud family. The conditions were highly dynamic and complex during that period, marked by tribal and political conflicts among various tribes in the region. There was also influence from British and Ottoman expansion in the area, as well as local religious and social influences. In the modern era before unification, Saudi Arabia was gradually expanding under the leadership of the ruling family in efforts to combat other tribes and unify the territories under its control. However, this process was ongoing and complex, leading to alliances and conflicts with various groups and tribes in the region.
