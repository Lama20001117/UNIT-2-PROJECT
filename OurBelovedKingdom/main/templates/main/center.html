The Central Region

Before the Saudi rule, the conditions in the regions

Especially in Najd:

Were distinct from the cosmopolitan coastal areas and holy sites. Najd, one of the least influenced regions by non-Arab populations in the Arabian Peninsula, predominantly comprised Arab tribes known for their lineage, generosity, and courage. Leadership was typically hereditary within families, with transitions occurring between different tribes. Notable leaders included the rulers of Al-'Uyaynah, Tharmada', Rawdat Sudayr, and Buraydah. Economically, Najd relied on local trade of agricultural and animal products, such as camel and sheep products like milk, ghee, wool, and hides. Additionally, there was regional trade with neighboring regions and foreign trade in goods like clothing, food, and weapons.
