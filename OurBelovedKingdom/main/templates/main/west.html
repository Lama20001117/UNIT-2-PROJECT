The Western Region

Before the Saudi rule, the conditions in the regions

Western Saudi Arabia:

"In the western region, Muhammad bin Sulaiman al-Hasani seized control of the Emirate of Mecca from the Abbasid governor in 301 AH/914 AD. The rule remained in the hands of the al-Hasani Sharifs until the Qarmatians took over in 317 AH/929 AD. Later, the leader Ja'far bin Muhammad al-Hasani managed to regain control over Mecca and its surroundings. The Hashemites from the al-Hasani Sharifs continued to govern until Qatada bin Idris took over the Emirate in 597 AH/1201 AD. This aristocratic class continued to rule the Hijaz until 1343 AH/1925 AD. The economic life in the Hijaz relied on animal wealth and its products, while the coastal populations depended on fishing resources. Economic life in the Hijaz was influenced by the general political situation. When political conditions stabilized, economic conditions improved. Conversely, if the political situation deteriorated, economic conditions worsened, leading to economic disasters."
