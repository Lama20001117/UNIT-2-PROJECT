The Eastern Region

Before the Saudi rule, the conditions in the regions

Eastern Saudi Arabia:

"The eastern part of the country was once referred to as 'Al Bahrain,' which included Al-Ahsa and Al-Qatif. The first Friday prayer outside the Prophet's Mosque was held at the Jowatha Mosque in that region. The area was inhabited by the Abdul Qais tribe, one of the earliest tribes to embrace Islam. The region was under the rule of the Qarmatians until Abdullah bin Ali Al-Ayouni from the tribe of Abdul Qais managed to overthrow the Qarmatian state with military assistance from the Abbasid state. The Al-Ayouni family continued to rule the eastern region until the middle of the seventh century Hijri, when the rule was seized from them by the Banu Amir tribe led by Asfur bin Rashid. They continued to rule the country until their rule was ended by Said bin Mughamis. Then the rule in the region was regained by the Banu Amir until the year 820 Hijri/1417 CE. The region then went through various ruling dynasties of the Banu Amir until it came under Ottoman rule for about 120 years. Eventually, the Al-Hameed leaders of the Bani Khalid succeeded in controlling the eastern region and taking over its rule. The Bani Khalid continued to rule until the region came under the rule of the first Saudi state. The economic life in the region depended on agriculture, trade, fishing, and pearl diving. The inhabitants of the region enjoyed more economic prosperity than other areas, as many people forced by living conditions in Najd migrated there."
